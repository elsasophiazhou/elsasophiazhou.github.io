Econ 115: Graduate Enrollment after the China Initiative
Labor China

Using synthetic control to construct a counterfactual for Chinese graduate enrollment at U.S. universities, this paper estimates that the DOJ's 2018 China Initiative caused a 4.05% drop in Chinese graduate enrollment, with a potentially accelerating trend.

Econ 122: Health after Insurance for China's Migrant Population
Health Labor China

Using a two-way fixed-effects difference-in-differences design on China Family Panel Studies data, this paper finds that China's 2016 merger of urban (URBMI) and rural (NRCMS) health insurance produced a net increase in uninsurance of 5.6 percentage points among migrants in the short run, consistent with individuals disenrolling from NRCMS upon announcement of the new scheme but facing delays in completing enrollment in URRBMI (the merged insurance scheme). This paper did not find significant changes in self-reported health outcomes or utilization.

Econ 177: Migration after Extreme Temperature under China's Hukou System
Health China

Using a two-way fixed-effects regression on China Panel Family Studies data aggregated to the province-year level, this paper finds that one more day where average temperature rises above 90°F raises inter-provincial migration rates by 0.19 percentage points, with the hukou system likely muting a larger response.

Soc 175: Control over Growth
China

China's initial growth model was built on capital accumulation, export manufacturing, and an extensive use of FDI. As investment returns diminish, the party's drive for political consolidation increasingly blocks the fiscal and structural reforms needed to avoid the middle-income trap.

Soc 175: Growth without Discipline
China

China's economy is organized differently from other major modern economies through its reliance on state-owned enterprises, financial insulation from the global economy, and a party promotion system narrowly focused on GDP growth and fiscal revenues. These features consolidate political power but generate runaway debt and slacken market discipline.
